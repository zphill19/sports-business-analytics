#  Executive Brief: Stadium Revenue Optimization Strategy
**Prepared for:** Front-Office Leadership | **Author:** Zachary Phillips

##  Strategic Objective
To evaluate upcoming scheduling bottlenecks and deploy a targeted marketing promotion framework across the final 81 unplayed games of the season to maximize stadium profitability.

##  Operational Findings & Recommendations
* **The Performance Ratio:** Initial machine learning regression models projected a baseline future split of **19 Blockbuster (Above Average) games** and **23 Underperforming (Below Average) games**.
* **The Low-Market Deficit Trap:** Diagnostic testing confirms that attempting to run revenue promotions against Low-Market rivals yields a negative return on investment (ROI). The underlying market demand gap is too large to overcome, making these dates an operational "lost cause."
* **Prescriptive Recommendation:** Management should focus resources away from lost causes and inject exactly **five strategic marketing promotions** into high-probability "Marginal Loser" home dates. This targeted framework pushes the team's operational ratio from **19/23 up to 24/18**.

##  Financial Cost Constraints & Realism
* **Margin Prioritization:** This strategy caps the prescriptive simulation at a conservative **1.10 revenue lift factor (10% bump)**. Because marketing giveaways (t-shirts, jerseys) introduce heavy fixed operational costs, aiming for lower, realistic targets protects team margins and ensures net profitability outpaces gross metrics.
